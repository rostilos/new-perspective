Why should You upgrade to Google Analytics 4

Google Analytics 4 is the 4th generation of Google Analytics used by merchants, agencies, and store owners to get detailed metrics and reports on how customers are interacting with their various products and services.

If you are Magento merchants selling products online and strive to attract new customers or improve your conversion rates, you definitely need Google Analytics 4. It is the instrument to gather as much information as possible about your customers behavior from your website since it is the only layer between your customers and your products.

The absolute values of goals and conversion events should not be compared across two resources, even if their settings are copied, as the statistics in the reports will differ due to the different approaches.

Traditional Google Analytics is based on session and page views and how people interact with your website. Google Analytics 4, instead, is based on events and parameters and designed to track users' interactions with your products by offering the ability to track:

  • Add to Cart
  • Remove from Cart
  • Product Impression
  • and much more

Users of Google Analytics can analyze and improve their conversion rate by using in-depth reports, metrics, and tools designed to help analyze and monitor their site's performance.

How to implement Google Analytics 4 with Magento

Although GA4 supports the Enhanced Ecommerce data schema, we recommend using the new data model instead of the old one as there is no information on how long the last one will be supported.

GA4 is not your typical software upgrade. It’s a completely different platform, and setting up GA4 on ecommerce sites is not as easy as flipping a toggle switch anymore.

The GTM container alone is not enough for ecommerce shops. To get started you will need to add a data layer - pieces of code, which provides products and purchases data transfer directly to Google Analytics.

That is why the shift to GA4 can be stressful and you need a developer assistance to carry out the data layer adding . Also Installation steps are far different from what we were used to with Universal Analytics.

Better tracking your customers journey

Instead of fragmented information based on individual devices and platforms, Google Analytics 4 provides customer-centric measurement. Now You can learn more about your customers at every stage of their lifetime, from acquisition to conversion to mobile app usage, regardless of how they interact with your brand.

Redefinding time-based events

Despite providing great insight into time spent on pages and sessions, Universal Analytics often fails to provide a deeper understanding of more specific actions you want your users to take on your site, such as watching the video you put together to cover your presentation. With GA4, you can define time measurements for defined actions or sets of actions using Ga4's "Elapsed Time" functionality. You can track how long it takes for a user to watch your video, read the Category Page, then complete the Checkout Process.

Next-level retargeting

Retargeting before GA4 was still extremely efficient, but lacked the dynamism of excluding certain users (temporarily or permanently) from being retargeted. With GA4, you can more efficiently use retargeting to, for example, prevent users who recently purchased an item from seeing the same advertisement until they're eligible for a new set of ads.
