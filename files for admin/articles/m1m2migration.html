In the summer of 2020, Adobe stopped supporting Magento 1. It means that the modules and extensions have ceased to be updated, and development for the M1 has become custom. This is accompanied by:


  • high probability of bugs

  • high service cost M1

  • the process of business scaling is complicated

The projects on M2 are more in demand in the world of e-commerce. In this article, we will consider what other factors influence the transition to M2 and what is so good new version of the platform.

by Yaroslav Zinchuk
 
 

Documentation chaos

Let’s imagine that the e-commerce project is 5-7 years old. As a rule, it started with one team of developers. Then there are several contractor changes. Often, such a project does not have documentation. Thus, it takes up to 3-4 months to understand and put the existing functionality in order. This information has been layered for years. Apart from it, there's custom functionality, which has been added many times. The list of modified and customized functionality does not exist. His audit might take a while.

As a result, the magento developer is dedicated to the website audit of the week, but this does not help to realize all the optimization tasks. Even installing a new version of PHP 7.2, which is also possible on the M1 platform, does not solve the problem. The broken functions need to be replaced. In our recent experience, it took more than 50 hours on one of the major e-commerce projects and 80 hours on the other.

Need of refactoring

Switching to the new M2 platform is an opportunity to redesign the functionality and refactor the code. Over the years, new business requirements have emerged. Migration allows you to adapt the project to today’s realities, and get rid of unnecessary.

Terms of migration: from 3 to 6 months. After all, migration is just like making a site from scratch. Migration includes:

  • revision of functionality and its refactoring
  • improvement of website usability
  • partial site redesign is possible
  • Data transfer.

Now about the main advantages of M2, for which the online store is worth migrating to the new platform.

  1. The first is speed.

    2. Your site’s pages will be downloaded at least 25% faster than before. Full Page Cache is built into the box. Optimizes performance and Varnish Cache. It will help to reduce the space on the server and save costs for cloud infrastructure. Thanks to the high speed will improve the conversion and opportunities for SEO-site promotion. Checkout in Magento 2 is 38% faster. The ability to take orders is increased by 117% compared to Magento 1.

  2. The second, which is pleasing, is the simplified procedure of installing additional modules when using Composer.

It enters the box itself M2. Its main task is to automatically tighten the package of extensions, which greatly simplifies their updating in the future. There is also a more «usable» administrative panel. Even an inexperienced user can manage an online store with an easy-to-understand interface. Another advantage of Magento 2 is the support of different payment options.

Who needs to migrate?

Migration - a matter of near-term prospects for both large and small online stores. For the latter to make the transition to M2 manages faster. The fewer data and functionality available, the easier it is to migrate to the new platform.

If a business wants to grow, it will have to use the features and modules that are being created on Magento 2 this year. In August 2022, a new version of Magento 2.4.5 was released. It contains even more useful functions in the «box», which were not in M1 and M2.
